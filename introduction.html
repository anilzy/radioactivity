Radioactive Decay – Introduction

The unstable atomic nuclei achieve stability by emitting α-particles, β-particles, or γ-rays.

This spontaneous emission of particles or radiation from unstable nuclei is known as Radioactivity.

Radioactivity is purely a nuclear phenomenon. The rate of emission depends only on the number of unstable nuclei and is unaffected by temperature, pressure, or chemical reactions.

Radioactivity was discovered accidentally in 1896 by Henri Becquerel.
